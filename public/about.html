What is Steganography?

Steganography is the practice of concealing information within another message or physical object. Unlike cryptography, which scrambles data to make it unreadable, steganography hides the very existence of the message.

Ancient Origins

Steganography dates back to ancient Greece, where messages were hidden on wooden tablets covered with wax, or tattooed on messengers' scalps.

Digital Evolution

Modern digital steganography hides information in images, audio, video, and other digital media using sophisticated algorithms.

Security Applications

Used by security professionals, journalists, and privacy advocates for covert communication and information protection.

LSB Technique

Our application uses Least Significant Bit (LSB) steganography to embed messages in image pixels with imperceptible changes.

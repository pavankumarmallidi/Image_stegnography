Privacy Policy

Your privacy is our top priority. Learn how we protect your data and respect your privacy.

Overview

At SteganoCrypt, your privacy is our fundamental commitment. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use our steganography application.

Key Principle: We process all data client-side. Your images and messages never leave your device during steganography operations.

Data Collection

What We DON'T Collect

  • Your uploaded images
  • Your secret messages
  • Encoded or decoded content
  • Personal files or documents

What We May Collect

  • Basic usage analytics (page views, session duration)
  • Error logs for debugging purposes
  • Contact form submissions (when you voluntarily provide information)

How We Protect Your Privacy

Client-Side Processing

All steganography operations happen entirely in your browser using JavaScript. Your data never leaves your device.

No Server Storage

We don't store any images, messages, or processed content on our servers. Everything is processed locally.

HTTPS Encryption

All communication between your browser and our servers is encrypted using industry-standard SSL/TLS protocols.

No Tracking

We don't use invasive tracking technologies or third-party analytics that compromise your privacy.

Your Rights

  • Right to know what data we collect
  • Right to access your personal information
  • Right to correct inaccurate information
  • Right to delete your information
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to opt-out of data processing

Cookies and Local Storage

We use minimal cookies and local storage only for essential functionality:

  • Session management
  • User preferences (theme, language)
  • Error logging for debugging

You can disable cookies in your browser settings, though some functionality may be limited.

Policy Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated revision date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
